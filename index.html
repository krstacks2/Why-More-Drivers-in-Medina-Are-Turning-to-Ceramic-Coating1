What is Ceramic Coating and How Does It Work?

Let’s keep it simple. Ceramic coating is a protective liquid polymer that’s applied to your vehicle’s exterior. When it bonds with your paint, it forms a clear layer that repels water, dirt, and grime. Think of it like a permanent shield, one that’s invisible to the eye but powerful in performance.

Instead of constantly waxing your car or worrying about minor surface marks, ceramic coating helps your vehicle maintain that showroom shine with minimal effort. It’s not magic, but it is real protection that you can see every time the sunlight hits your hood.

Ceramic Coating vs Paint Correction: Do You Need Both?

A lot of first-time clients ask us if they need paint correction before applying ceramic coating. The short answer? Not always, but it helps.

If your car’s paint has swirl marks, oxidation, or light scratches, we usually recommend correcting those flaws before sealing the surface. Otherwise, you’re locking in imperfections.

Our technicians will inspect your vehicle carefully and walk you through the options. If you need correction, we’ll take care of it. If your paint is already in great shape, we’ll move straight into coating. No upsells. No pressure. Just honest recommendations.

Maintenance Tips After Getting Ceramic Coating

Once your ceramic coating is applied, taking care of your vehicle becomes much easier, but there are still a few things to keep in mind:

Use pH-neutral soaps when washing.
Avoid automated car washes with brushes.
Wash by hand with microfiber mitts when possible.
Avoid waxing or polishing, as these can interfere with the coating.

We’ll provide you with a simple aftercare guide so you can maintain that slick, glossy look long after your appointment.
